## 1. Brand Concept

**Name:** Motor City Math

**Design inspiration:** Detroit Pistons brand language â€” bold, confident, physical. This isn't a gentle pastel ed-tech app. It's a study tool with attitude, built by a dad who treats his son's grades the way Detroit treats basketball: seriously, with grit, and with zero excuses.

**Pistons context:** Cade Cunningham's breakout All-Star season, Jaden Ivey's comeback, a 30-win improvement â€” the Pistons rebuild is a parallel to Kai's grade trajectory. From struggling to dominant. That's the energy.

**Tagline options:**
- "Put in the work."
- "No shortcuts. Just reps."
- "Grind. Learn. Win."

**The story:** Marcus built 12 HTML practice tests and helped Kai go from struggling to earning an A in Algebra II at Seattle Academy (SAAS). Now we're building the system that makes that repeatable â€” adaptive tests, spaced repetition, per-standard tracking â€” all powered by a 6-agent AI team.

**School context:** Seattle Academy of Arts & Sciences (SAAS). Cardinals mascot, red/black/white school colors. Standards-based grading. Mission: Question, Imagine, Create, Contribute. The tests already reference SAAS standards (3.a, 3.b, etc.).

## 1b. ADHD Design Constraints *(Mash-approved)*

Kai has ADHD. Every screen must respect three principles:

1. **Focus over features** â€” During tests, show only the question, input, and timer. Hide everything else. No sidebar, no nav, no unrelated CTAs.
2. **Encourage over evaluate** â€” Use coach tone ("You nailed 12 out of 15") not clinical ("Score: 80%"). Max 12 words per feedback line.
3. **One thing at a time** â€” Reduce choices to prevent decision paralysis. One CTA per screen. Progressive disclosure on dashboard â€” show overview first, drill-down on tap.

**Hard rules:**
- Timer shows time *remaining*, not elapsed
- Show progress position ("Question 5 of 20") always visible
- No walls of text â€” ever
- Feedback copy must be â‰¤ 12 words

## 8. Math Rendering

| | MathJax | KaTeX |
|-|---------|-------|
| Render speed | ~500ms | ~50ms (10x faster) |
| Bundle size | 1.2MB | 200KB |
| Visual quality | Excellent | Excellent |
| Offline | Needs CDN or big local copy | Smaller local footprint |

Current state: 4/12 files use MathJax, 8 use plain text. Moving to KaTeX everywhere gives fast rendering with clean LaTeX notation for all equations.
